Privacy Policy — ChessUnlock

Developer: Aimlessoul

Last updated: 2026-05-19

Summary: ChessUnlock is an app-locker that blocks selected apps until you solve a chess puzzle. Most lock-related data stays on your device. The app uses third-party services including Lichess for puzzles, Google AdMob for ads, Firebase Analytics for app usage analytics, and Firebase Crashlytics for crash reporting. We do not sell your personal data.

1) What ChessUnlock does

ChessUnlock lets you select apps to lock. When you try to open a locked app while locked mode is active, ChessUnlock shows an on-screen blocking overlay and lets you unlock by solving a chess puzzle.

The app may also let you continue solving extra puzzles after unlocking, choose an unlock time, use hints or skips, and send feedback.

2) Information we collect

We do not intentionally collect personal information like your name, phone number, contacts, or precise location.

2.1 Information stored locally on your device

The app stores certain data locally on your device so the app can work correctly:

  • Locked apps list such as package names, app names, and app icons, so ChessUnlock knows which apps you chose to lock.
  • Unlock state such as unlock timer, lock status, or unlock-until timestamp.
  • Settings such as puzzle difficulty/rating level, theme, and app preferences.
  • Basic local statistics such as solved puzzle count, best puzzle rating, and accuracy/first-try stats.
  • Onboarding status so the app knows whether to show first-time setup guidance.

This lock-related information is stored locally using on-device storage such as Android SharedPreferences or similar local storage. We do not upload your exact locked apps list to our own server.

2.2 Analytics information

ChessUnlock uses Firebase Analytics to understand how the app is used and to improve the app experience. Analytics may collect app usage events such as:

  • App opened or onboarding shown.
  • Locked apps saved, using only safe counts such as the number of locked apps.
  • Overlay shown and Open ChessUnlock button tapped.
  • Puzzle started, puzzle solved, Solve another tapped, and Unlock apps tapped.
  • Hint or Skip interactions and ad reward/failure events.
  • Difficulty/rating level changes.
  • Unlock time selected.
  • Feedback option opened.

We do not intentionally send your exact locked app names, package names, contacts, email, phone number, or full installed app list through Analytics.

Firebase Analytics may also collect data such as number of users, session statistics, approximate location, browser/device information, app-instance identifiers, and advertising identifiers where available.

2.3 Crash and diagnostic information

ChessUnlock uses Firebase Crashlytics to detect, diagnose, and fix crashes and stability issues. Crash reports may include:

  • Crash stack traces.
  • Relevant app state at the time of the crash.
  • Device metadata such as device model, operating system version, and app version.
  • Crashlytics installation identifiers.
  • Breadcrumb logs showing recent app actions before a crash.

Crashlytics is used only to improve app stability and fix bugs.

2.4 Advertising information

ChessUnlock uses Google AdMob to show ads, including banner ads and rewarded ads for features such as Hint and Skip. AdMob and the Google Mobile Ads SDK may collect and share data such as:

  • IP address, which may be used to estimate general location.
  • User product interactions such as app launches, taps, ad impressions, and video views.
  • Diagnostic information related to app and SDK performance.
  • Device and account identifiers such as Android Advertising ID, app set ID, and related identifiers where available.

This data may be used for advertising, analytics, fraud prevention, and improving ad performance.

2.5 Feedback information

If you choose to send feedback, we may receive the information you voluntarily provide, such as your message, device details you include, screenshots you attach, or your email address if you contact us by email. Feedback is optional.

3) Permissions and why we need them

ChessUnlock requests certain permissions so it can work as an app locker:

  • Usage Access / Usage Stats: To detect which app is currently in the foreground, so ChessUnlock can lock selected apps when needed.
  • Display over other apps / Overlay: To show a blocking screen over a locked app until you solve a puzzle or unlock.
  • Foreground Service: To keep the app watcher running reliably so the lock can be enforced.
  • Notifications: To show the required persistent notification while the foreground service is running, where required by Android.
  • Internet: To fetch chess puzzles, load ads, send analytics events, report crashes, and open feedback links.
  • Advertising ID: Used by Google AdMob and Google/Firebase services for ads, analytics, and measurement where available.
  • Receive Boot Completed if enabled: To restart the watcher after device reboot so locking can resume. Some device brands may restrict auto-start; you may need to allow auto-start or battery exceptions for consistent behavior.

4) Third-party services

4.1 Lichess puzzle API

ChessUnlock fetches chess puzzles from Lichess using their public puzzle endpoint. This requires an internet connection. We do not control Lichess, and their servers may log standard request data such as IP address as part of normal web operations.

Lichess API reference: https://lichess.org/api/puzzle/next

4.2 Google AdMob

ChessUnlock uses Google AdMob to show ads. AdMob may collect and share information such as ad interactions, device identifiers, IP address, diagnostic information, and Advertising ID where available. This is used for advertising, analytics, measurement, and fraud prevention.

You can reset or delete your Android Advertising ID or manage ad personalization from your device settings where supported by Android.

4.3 Firebase Analytics

ChessUnlock uses Firebase Analytics to understand general app usage and improve the app. We use Analytics events to understand things like whether users complete onboarding, solve puzzles, use hints/skips, continue solving puzzles, and unlock apps. We do not intentionally send exact locked app names or package names through Analytics.

4.4 Firebase Crashlytics

ChessUnlock uses Firebase Crashlytics to receive crash reports and diagnostic information. This helps us find and fix bugs, especially device-specific issues related to overlays, usage access, app locking, ads, and puzzle loading.

4.5 Feedback services

If the app opens an email app or feedback form, the information you submit is voluntary. If a third-party form service is used, that service may process the information you submit according to its own privacy policy.

5) How we use information

  • To enforce app locking and unlock timers.
  • To show your settings and local progress statistics.
  • To fetch chess puzzles from Lichess.
  • To show ads and rewarded ads through AdMob.
  • To understand app usage and improve the user experience.
  • To detect, diagnose, and fix crashes and bugs.
  • To respond to feedback if you contact us.

6) Data sharing

We do not sell your personal data.

Your exact locked apps list and local puzzle statistics are stored on your device and are not uploaded to our own server.

Some data is processed by third-party services used in the app:

  • Lichess receives puzzle API requests and may process standard request data such as IP address.
  • Google AdMob processes ad-related data for advertising, analytics, measurement, and fraud prevention.
  • Firebase Analytics processes app usage analytics events and related device/app information.
  • Firebase Crashlytics processes crash reports and diagnostic data.
  • Feedback providers may process information you voluntarily submit through email or a feedback form.

7) Data retention and deletion

Local app data can be removed at any time by:

  • Clearing the app’s storage in Android settings, or
  • Uninstalling the app.

Crash, analytics, advertising, and feedback data handled by third-party services may be retained according to those services’ policies and settings.

You can reset or delete your Android Advertising ID or manage ad personalization in your Android device settings where supported.

If you contact us by email and want your feedback message deleted, you can request deletion using the contact email below.

8) Security

We aim to keep ChessUnlock minimal and store lock-related data locally where possible. Firebase services encrypt data in transit using HTTPS and some Firebase services also encrypt data at rest. No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure.

9) Children’s privacy

ChessUnlock is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through feedback or another channel, contact us so we can review and delete it where appropriate.

10) Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as the app evolves, including when we add, remove, or change third-party services, analytics, ads, crash reporting, or feedback features. We will update the “Last updated” date above when this policy changes.
